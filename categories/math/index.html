Winning the Olympics

Published: Fri 24 April 2020
Updated: Sat 30 May 2020
By Julian Berman

In math.

This video has an interesting showerthought about how to define who wins the olympics.

In short, rather than absolute number of medals (which favors large nations) or medals per capita (which favors small nations), a probabilistic-y combination of them is proposed (that’s obviously the technical term for it. Essentially it’s calculating which nations did better than expected probabilistically given their size).

Absolutely imperfect, but fun to think about.
